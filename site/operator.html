Levers you can pull now

The engine — how it spreads

Growth here isn't ads; it's the scarcity and the ritual. The switches already built into the product:

  • Hand-to-hand codes. Every code is a physical, named object. Track each one in the ledger above so the “100 founders” stays an honest number.
  • First users get 3 codes each. The invited become inviters — the referral graph without a growth-hack smell. (In-game: earned by recognition.)
  • Shareable postcards. The game emits a canvas postcard carrying the player's last real French phrase — the viral loop's evidence. (v0.6 “Carte Postale”.)
  • The Thursday ritual. Dolan's Pub, 18h–20h, glowing in-game with a live countdown — “you had to be there,” with an address.
  • The city relights. Each stamp visibly restores the dark map; milestones at 5/10/18/26 — visible collective progress.

The founders'-100 playbook (staged, Harvard-dorm style)

  1. Seed one perfect table. Run a single flawless expedition (the kit in expedition.html). Photograph it. That's your proof.
  2. Hand out the first ~20 codes to real people, logged here. Hôtes first — they unlock everyone else.
  3. Let scarcity work. Publish only true counts (“37 of the founding 100 spoken for”). The Competition Act likes honest numbers; so does trust.
  4. Open a region when a threshold is truly hit (the in-game province map). Moncton unlocks when Moncton earns it — never before.
  5. Only then, widen the mouth of the funnel (press, socials) — once the table is warm, not before.

One honest caveat before you say yes. Your published privacy page currently promises “No account. No tracking. It's a normal thing for a real business to do — but it means updating the privacy/terms pages to tell the truth about it. That's why I'm not switching it on without your say-so.
