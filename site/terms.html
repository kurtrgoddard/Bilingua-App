What this is / Ce que c'est

  • Bilingua Quest is a community prototype — a browser game and, eventually, real-world group activities — made in Fredericton, New Brunswick to help adults practice French. The game is free, forever. It is provided as-is, with love and without warranties.
  • Expeditions (real-world events): your first is always free. Afterward, pilot pricing is $5.00 CAD per seat (or a 10-seat carnet for $40), all-in — no hidden fees. 65% goes to your Hôte, 15% to a host-governed community fund. Payments are currently in test mode (no real charges) until the paid pilot opens; every scarcity or price number we publish is literally true.
  • It is for adults (18+). Real-world gatherings that end at licensed venues are 19+ (New Brunswick's drinking age).
  • It is a game, not a school, an employer, or a dating service. Be kind; be patient with learners; anything else forfeits your seat at the table.

Real places, real people / Vrais lieux, vraies personnes

  • Real Fredericton venues appear by name, with affection and without affiliation or endorsement. All characters are fictional; no character depicts real staff. Venue owners: we'll change or remove anything on request — one email is enough.
  • Trademarks and trade names belong to their owners. Our use is referential.

Real-world safety / La sécurité dans le vrai monde

  • If you attend a real gathering: meet in public places, tell a friend, trust your instincts, and leave whenever you want — no explanation needed.
  • Community events are volunteer-run, free, and low-risk by design (walking, talking, coffee) — but walking in a real city has ordinary risks (weather, surfaces, traffic), and you take part at your own judgment. Event-specific acknowledgments are signed at check-in.
  • We never share your contact information with other participants; exchanging contact is always your own choice, person to person.

Reserved tables / Les tables réservées

  • A reserved table is a small hosted conversation (one francophone Hôte + 3–5 learners) at a public venue. Reserving a seat stores your name and email so a human can confirm you — see Privacy.
  • While tables are free (the pilot), cancelling is one email — but please do cancel if you can't come: seats are few and honestly counted. A refund policy will be written into this page before any paid table exists.
  • Reserving a seat reserves a seat, not a person's attention or a lesson. The Hôte's role is to keep the table warmly in French; correction happens only by your consent.
  • We may cancel a table (weather, host illness); you'll be emailed as soon as we know, and a free-table cancellation is never a cost to you.

The invitation system / Les invitations

  • Invitation codes are free, personal, and have no cash value. Scarcity numbers we publish (like "the founding 100") are always literally true.
  • Don't scrape, resell, or mass-generate codes — it's not illegal cleverness we mind, it's that it makes the party worse.
