## Topic 1 — Using real business names (Dolan's Pub, Jonnie Java Roasters, etc.) in the game

### 1.1 Infringement (Trademarks Act ss. 19–20) requires "use as a trademark" — in-world depiction likely isn't that
- **Finding:** Ss. 19/20 infringement depends on "use" under s. 4 — the mark must be used to distinguish the defendant's *own* goods/services (the *Clairol International v Thomas Supply* (1968, Ex. Ct.) doctrine). A pub name on a pixel-art storefront inside a game narrative is referential/atmospheric content, not use of the mark as a brand for the game.
- **Sources:** Trademarks Act, RSC 1985, c T-13, ss. 4, 19, 20, 22 — [Justice Laws](https://laws-lois.justice.gc.ca/eng/acts/t-13/FullText.html) (acc. 2026-07-14); INTA, ["Clairol Revisited," The Trademark Reporter Vol. 110 No. 5 (2020)](https://www.inta.org/wp-content/uploads/public-files/resources/the-trademark-reporter/TMR-Vol-110-No-05-102920-Secured.pdf); Osler, ["'Use' it or lose it"](https://www.osler.com/en/insights/updates/use-it-or-lose-it-demystifying-trademark-use-impact-rights/).
- **Implication:** The strongest structural protection: Bilingua Quest is not selling pub/coffee services under those marks, and no reasonable player thinks the game *is* the pub.
- **Action (HIGH):** Keep real names strictly in-world. Never put them in the game's title, logo, store-listing keywords, or marketing hooks ("Featuring Dolan's Pub!") — marketing use edges toward s. 4 "use" and implied endorsement.

### 1.2 Depreciation of goodwill (s. 22) — the realistic theory, but narrow
- **Finding:** *Veuve Clicquot Ponsardin v Boutiques Cliquot Ltée*, 2006 SCC 23 sets a 4-part test: (1) use of the registered mark (or something evoking it), (2) significant goodwill, (3) likely mental linkage, (4) **likely depreciation** (tarnishment/dilution) — the claim failed even for a famous champagne mark absent evidence of likely damage. In *Michelin v CAW*, 1996 CanLII 11755 (FC), trademark claims failed on the "use" threshold even though the union reproduced Michelin's name and logo (Michelin won only on **copyright** in the drawing). In *BCAA v OPEIU*, 2001 BCSC 156, use of BCAA marks on a criticism website was neither passing off nor s. 22 depreciation. Counter-caution: *Source Perrier v Fira-Less Marketing* (1983 FCTD, "Pierre Eh!") shows Canadian courts will enjoin commercial spoofs trading on goodwill — Canada gives **less** deference to expressive use than the US. **No Canadian case squarely on real venues depicted in a video game was found** (gap; absence of authority UNVERIFIED as exhaustive).
- **Sources:** [Veuve Clicquot, 2006 SCC 23 (CanLII)](https://www.canlii.org/en/ca/scc/doc/2006/2006scc23/2006scc23.html); [Smart & Biggar case note](https://www.smartbiggar.ca/insights/publication/barbie-and-veuve-clicquot----supreme-court-of-canada-clarifies-protection-for-famous-trademarks-in-canada); [Michelin v CAW (CanLII)](https://www.canlii.org/en/ca/fct/doc/1996/1996canlii11755/1996canlii11755.html); [BCAA v OPEIU (vLex)](https://ca.vlex.com/vid/bcaa-v-opeiu-681640661); [INTA TMR Vol. 110 No. 5 (Bereskin) on parody](https://www.inta.org/wp-content/uploads/public-files/resources/the-trademark-reporter/TMR-Vol-110-No-05_Bereskin-1.pdf). All acc. 2026-07-14.
- **Implication:** Affectionate, non-disparaging depiction makes element (4) nearly impossible; s. 22 also applies only to **registered** marks, and most Fredericton small businesses likely hold unregistered ones. Risk rises only if a named real venue hosts negative in-game content (crime, mockery, health-code jokes, drunkenness).
- **Action (HIGH):** Editorial rule: nothing unsavoury ever happens at a named real venue; use fictional names for any such location.

### 1.3 Passing off (s. 7(b)/common law)
- **Finding:** *Ciba-Geigy Canada v Apotex*, [1992] 3 SCR 120: goodwill + public deception via misrepresentation (as to source/licence/endorsement) + damage. (Core holding standard; pincite not re-fetched — cite-check. Secondary: [Robic litigation overview](https://www.robic.ca/wp-content/uploads/2017/05/354E-HGR-2006.pdf).)
- **Implication:** A prominent no-affiliation disclaimer plus free/non-commercial context makes misrepresentation hard to establish. Residual risk is relational, not litigational.
- **Action (HIGH):** Draw **original pixel art**; never trace or reproduce business logos/signage artwork (logo drawings are copyright-protected — the *Michelin* lesson). Plain-text names are safer than reproduced logos.

### 1.4 US analogues — context only, clearly labelled US LAW
- **Finding (US):** *Rogers v Grimaldi* (2d Cir. 1989) protects trademark use in expressive works; *AM General v Activision* (SDNY 2020) applied it to real Humvees in Call of Duty; *Jack Daniel's v VIP Products* (US SCC 2023) narrows Rogers where the mark is the defendant's own source identifier. (Well-known holdings; not re-fetched — UNVERIFIED this session.)
- **Implication:** **No Canadian *Rogers* equivalent exists.** Do not rely on expressive-use defences in Canada; rely on the s. 4 "use" threshold, no confusion, no depreciation.
- **Action (LOW):** None beyond 1.1–1.3.

### 1.5 Disclaimer + goodwill letters + opt-out (industry convention, not case-law-mandated)
- **Recommended disclaimer (EN; produce FR version):** *"Bilingua Quest is an independent, non-commercial tribute to Fredericton, New Brunswick. All business names, trademarks, and trade names referenced belong to their respective owners. Their appearance is for atmospheric and cultural purposes only and does not imply any affiliation, sponsorship, or endorsement. Any owner who prefers not to be depicted may contact [email] and the reference will be removed in the next update."*
- **Action (HIGH — cheapest, highest-value step in this memo):** (1) Disclaimer in credits, website footer, store listing. (2) Friendly pre-launch letters/emails to each named business ("pixel love letter — say the word and we'll adjust/remove"). (3) Honour removal requests promptly; keep a dated log of consents/requests (written acquiescence evidence + marketing goodwill).

## Topic 3 — Competition Act / honest scarcity marketing

### 3.1 Framework and scope — applies even to a free product, but exposure is minimal
- **Finding:** S. 74.01(1)(a) covers false/misleading material representations made "for the purpose of promoting … the supply or use of a product or … **any business interest**, by any means whatever" — so invite marketing for a free game is technically in scope; the **general impression** as well as literal meaning is assessed (ss. 74.03(5), 52(4)). Criminal parallel (s. 52) needs knowledge/recklessness. Post-2022 civil AMPs: individuals up to greater of $750K and 3× benefit — but with zero revenue, 3× benefit ≈ $0; the real stake is reputational/prohibition orders. Since **June 20, 2025** (Bill C-59), private parties can seek Tribunal leave to bring deceptive-marketing claims (vanishingly unlikely here).
- **Sources:** [Competition Act s. 74.01](https://laws-lois.justice.gc.ca/eng/acts/C-34/section-74.01.html) (acc. 2026-07-14); [Competition Bureau, Guide to the 2022 amendments](https://competition-bureau.canada.ca/en/guide-2022-amendments-competition-act); [Fasken, private rights of action in force (June 2025)](https://www.fasken.com/en/knowledge/2025/06/new-private-rights-of-action-have-come-into-force).
- **Implication:** Honest marketing discipline is the whole compliance posture; the cost is zero.

### 3.2 Drip pricing — codified 2022; landmark Cineplex enforcement (verified)
- **Finding:** Bill C-19 (royal assent **2022-06-23**) added s. 74.01(1.1)/s. 52(1.3): advertising a price unattainable due to mandatory non-government fees is deemed misleading (government charges like sales tax excepted). *Commissioner v Cineplex* (Tribunal, announced **2024-09-23**): mandatory undisclosed $1.50 online booking fee = drip pricing; AMP **$38.9M+** (≈ fees collected June 2022–Dec 2023) + 10-year prohibition order; **Federal Court of Appeal dismissed Cineplex's appeal 2026-01-21**. Predecessors: Ticketmaster consent agreement 2019 ($4M + $500K costs); Amazon.ca 2017 ($1M — misleading savings claims, not drip pricing).
- **Sources:** [Competition Bureau news, Cineplex win (2024-09-23)](https://www.canada.ca/en/competition-bureau/news/2024/09/competition-bureau-wins-deceptive-marketing-case-against-cineplex.html); [Canadian Advertising Law blog, FCA upholds Cineplex decision (Jan 2026)](https://www.canadianadvertisinglaw.com/federal-court-of-appeal-upholds-competition-tribunal-decision-in-38-9-million-cineplex-online-ticket-price-case/); [Miller Thomson drip-pricing note](https://www.millerthomson.com/en/insights/competition-antitrust-foreign-investment/drip-pricing-canada-competition-act/); [Bureau, Ticketmaster release (2019-06-27)](https://www.canada.ca/en/competition-bureau/news/2019/06/ticketmaster-to-pay-45-million-to-settle-misleading-pricing-case.html); [Bureau, Amazon release (2017-01-11)](https://www.canada.ca/en/competition-bureau/news/2017/01/amazon-changes-pricing-practices-pays-1-1-million-settle-price-advertising-case.html).
- **Implication:** Drip pricing is irrelevant while free. If a paid tier ever launches, show the all-in price up front. **Priority: Low now / High if monetized.**

### 3.3 False scarcity / urgency — Bureau guidance directly on point; honest claims are fine
- **Finding:** The Bureau's **Deceptive Marketing Practices Digest, Vol. 6 (2023-04-17)** addresses scarcity cues and drip pricing: businesses may advertise limited availability, "but they should not do this if the information is not accurate," and **even a technically true scarcity cue can mislead via general impression**. The Bureau's ["Fake urgency cues" page](https://competition-bureau.canada.ca/en/deceptive-marketing-practices/types-deceptive-marketing-practices/fake-urgency-cues) flags: countdown timers that expire/reset while the offer continues; static "Only 2 left in stock" claims; fabricated demand ("5 other shoppers are looking"); "limited time" deals that quietly persist (page content confirmed via search excerpts; exact wording partially UNVERIFIED — direct fetch blocked). International taxonomy: [OECD, *Dark Commercial Patterns* (Oct 2022)](https://www.oecd.org/en/publications/dark-commercial-patterns_44f5e846-en.html); [ICPEN Dark Patterns Sweep report (July 2024)](https://www.icpen.org/sites/default/files/2024-07/Public%20Report%20ICPEN%20Dark%20Patterns%20Sweep.pdf). General-impression standard per *Richard v Time Inc.*, [2012 SCC 8](https://www.canlii.org/en/ca/scc/doc/2012/2012scc8/2012scc8.html) (credulous, inexperienced, hurried consumer) — Quebec CPA case but the standard is codified in the Competition Act and routinely cited federally (including in Cineplex).
- **Safe:** "We printed exactly 100 founding invitations — when they're gone, this batch is gone" (true, provable). A later, clearly labelled "Wave 2 — another 100" is fine because the original claim stays true.
- **Crosses the line:** fake/resetting countdowns; "only 3 left" when untrue or never-changing; "going fast!" without factual basis; silently reprinting "the founding 100"; fabricated demand counters; implying the game will become paid/closed if undecided.
- **Actions (HIGH, all $0):** (1) Keep proof exactly 100 were printed (print-shop invoice, photo) — substantiation is the defence. (2) Any displayed counter must track real redemptions. (3) Never reuse "founding batch" for later waves. (4) No countdown timers unless tied to a real, enforced deadline. (5) Review copy for overall impression, not just sentence-level truth.

## Topic 4 — Accessibility obligations

### 4.1 Accessible Canada Act — does NOT apply
- **Finding:** The ACA (SC 2019, c 10) covers **federally regulated entities** only (federal government, Crown corps, banks, telecom/broadcast, interprovincial transport, postal). A provincially situated private individual's free web app is not covered. New federal digital-accessibility regulations (SOR/2025-255, Canada Gazette Part II, 2025-12-17) even exempt small federal-jurisdiction entities (≤99 employees) from Phase 1.
- **Sources:** [ESDC, Summary of the ACA](https://www.canada.ca/en/employment-social-development/programs/accessible-canada/act-summary.html); [Canada Gazette Part II, SOR/2025-255 (2025-12-17)](https://gazette.gc.ca/rp-pr/p2/2025/2025-12-17/html/sor-dors255-eng.html); [Level Access, Canadian accessibility laws guide](https://www.levelaccess.com/blog/canadian-accessibility-laws/). Acc. 2026-07-14.
- **Action:** None required (informational).

### 4.2 NB Accessibility Act — passed 2024, no private-sector standards yet (as of 2026-07)
- **Finding:** **Accessibility Act, SNB 2024, c 27**, royal assent **2024-06-07** (introduced as **Bill 47**, 60th Legislature — not "Bill 30"). Framework statute targeting an accessible NB by **2040**; Accessibility Advisory Board appointed Aug 2024; first five-year **Strategic Plan on Accessibility** released 2025, naming first three standard-development priorities: **Information and Communication, Employment, Built Environment**; Feb 2026 MOU between Accessibility Standards Canada and NB on joint standards development. **No accessibility-standards regulations bind private organizations yet.** Comparators (Manitoba 2013, Nova Scotia 2017) show private-sector web standards arrive years after the framework act (comparator detail UNVERIFIED at primary-source level).
- **Sources:** [Accessibility Act, SNB 2024, c 27 (CanLII)](https://www.canlii.org/en/nb/laws/astat/snb-2024-c-27/latest/snb-2024-c-27.html); [Legislative Assembly of NB, Bill 47](https://www.legnb.ca/en/legislation/bills/60/3/47/accessibility-act); [GNB Strategic Plan on Accessibility](https://www.gnb.ca/en/campaign/accessibility/strategic-plan.html); [ASC–NB MOU news release (Feb 2026)](https://www.canada.ca/en/accessibility-standards-canada/news/2026/02/accessibility-standards-canada-and-new-brunswick-join-forces-on-accessibility-standards-development.html); [Cox & Palmer note](https://coxandpalmerlaw.com/publication/the-new-brunswick-accessibility-act-potential-responsibilities-for-employers/). ### 4.3 NB Human Rights Act — the live legal hook today
- **Finding:** Human Rights Act, RSNB 2011, c 171 prohibits disability discrimination in **services offered to the public** and imposes a duty to accommodate to undue hardship; complaints to the NB Human Rights Commission (generally within 12 months). A public web game is plausibly a "service"; an inaccessible one could ground a complaint even absent an accessibility statute (cf. federal *Jodhan* reasoning; no NB web-service case found — UNVERIFIED).
- **Sources:** [RSNB 2011, c 171 (CanLII)](https://www.canlii.org/en/nb/laws/stat/rsnb-2011-c-171/latest/rsnb-2011-c-171.html); [NBHRC, Act explained](https://www2.gnb.ca/content/gnb/en/departments/nbhrc/human-rights-act/act-explained.html).
- **Action (MED):** Provide an accessibility-feedback contact channel and respond to accommodation requests in good faith.

### 4.4 Plain language — Grade 8 claim, correctly attributed (important correction)
- **Finding:** Accessibility Standards Canada's **CAN-ASC-3.1:2025 – Plain Language** (Canada's first National Standard of Canada on plain language, published **Oct 2025**, free EN/FR) does **NOT** prescribe Grade 8 — it defines plain language functionally (find / understand / use) and cautions that readability formulas and grade levels are unreliable. The Grade-8 figure belongs to the **Canada.ca Content Style Guide** (aim for grade 8 or lower for general audiences) and the TBS *Guideline on Making Communications Products and Activities Accessible* historically required max Grade 8 — and CDS's Oct 2024 update moved federal guidance away from rigid readability scores. So: "ASC recommends Grade 8" is **incorrect as attributed**; cite Canada.ca Style Guide for the number, CAN-ASC-3.1 for the method.
- **Sources:** [ASC, CAN-ASC-3.1:2025](https://accessible.canada.ca/creating-accessibility-standards/can-asc-312025-plain-language); [ASC news release (Oct 2025)](https://accessible.canada.ca/news/accessibility-standards-canada-publishes-first-plain-language-standard); [Canada.ca Content Style Guide](https://design.canada.ca/style-guide/); [CDS blog (2024-10-17)](https://digital.canada.ca/2024/10/17/plain-language-updates-to-the-canada.ca-content-style-guide/); [TBS Guideline (PDF)](https://publications.gc.ca/collections/collection_2022/sct-tbs/BT39-71-2022-eng.pdf). Acc. 2026-07-14.
- **Action (MED):** Write UI text plainly in both languages using CAN-ASC-3.1 as the design reference; attribute any "Grade 8" target to the Canada.ca guide.

### 4.5 WCAG 2.2 AA — voluntary best-practice target
- **Finding:** WCAG 2.2 (W3C Recommendation, 2023-10-05 — date UNVERIFIED at source this session) is backwards-compatible; 2.2 AA conformance also satisfies 2.1/2.0 AA. Canadian legal references remain older (AODA: WCAG 2.0 AA; federal EN 301 549: WCAG 2.1 AA; NB: none). **WCAG 2.2 AA therefore meets or exceeds every current Canadian legal benchmark.**
- **Sources:** [W3C WCAG 2.2](https://www.w3.org/TR/WCAG22/); [Level Access guide](https://www.levelaccess.com/blog/canadian-accessibility-laws/); [Siteimprove overview](https://www.siteimprove.com/blog/a-complete-overview-of-canadas-accessibility-laws/).
- **Action (HIGH for a new build):** Adopt WCAG 2.2 AA (keyboard nav, contrast, focus indicators, correct `lang` attributes for EN/FR content) and publish an accessibility statement — future-proofs against NB standards and strengthens grant applications.

## Topic 6 — Wolastoqey naming and land acknowledgment

### 6.1 Using "Wolastoq" — respectful, established public usage
- **Finding:** Wolastoq = "beautiful and bountiful river"; **Wolastoqiyik** = the people; **Wolastoqey** = adjective/language. ### 6.2 Renaming status — NOT officially renamed

### 6.3 Land acknowledgment — UNB reference wording
- **Finding:** UNB's acknowledgment elements (consistently attested; single-sentence verbatim UNVERIFIED since unb.ca fetch was blocked): UNB "stands on the **unsurrendered and unceded** traditional lands of **Wolastoqiyik (Maliseet)**"; the territory is covered by the **Treaties of Peace and Friendship first signed with the British Crown in 1725**; the treaties "**did not deal with surrender of lands and resources** but in fact recognized Wolastoqey (Maliseet), Mi'kmaq and Passamaquoddy title." ### 6.4 Respectful naming vs. appropriation — the line

## Topic 7 — Nonprofit structure

### 7.1 NB regime: still the Companies Act — no new NB NFP Act found (important correction to the brief)
- **Finding:** As of 2026-07-14 searches, NB not-for-profit companies still incorporate under **Part II of the Companies Act, RSNB 1973, c C-13** (letters-patent style, **minimum 3 applicants aged 19+**). No "NB Not-for-Profit Corporations Act (2022)" could be found on legnb.ca, CanLII, SNB, or Atlantic firm bulletins — the premise appears to be a **conflation with Saskatchewan's Non-profit Corporations Act, 2022** (UNVERIFIED as the source of confusion). NB's 2022 activity was Bill 24 modernizing the **Business Corporations Act** (for-profit), which incidentally allows a BCA corporation to continue under the Companies Act as a non-profit.
- **Sources:** [SNB Corporate Registry, Not-for-Profit Companies](https://www2.snb.ca/content/snb/en/sites/corporate-registry/not-for-profit-companies.html) (excerpt-verified; direct fetch 403); [GNB, Incorporation of Non-Profit Companies](https://www2.gnb.ca/content/gnb/en/services/services_renderer.201449.Incorporation_of_Non-Profit_Companies.html); [Legislative Assembly of NB, Bill 24](https://www.legnb.ca/en/legislation/bills/60/2/24/an-act-to-amend-the-business-corporations-act); [Stewart McKelvey](https://stewartmckelvey.com/thought-leadership/significant-amendments-to-the-business-corporations-act-new-brunswick-proposed/); [McInnes Cooper](https://www.mcinnescooper.com/publications/new-brunswick-business-corporations-act-10-key-proposed-amendments/). Acc. 2026-07-14.
- **Cost:** NB incorporation fee is property-scaled: **$62** (property ≤ $25K — the tier for an asset-light game), $112 ($25–50K), $162 ($50–75K); annual return via Form 35.1 (annual fee UNVERIFIED, historically ~$32–60). Sources: GNB service page above; [SNB NFP incorporation PDF](https://www.pxw1.snb.ca/snb7001/b/1000/CSS-FOL-SNB-45-0007B.pdf); [SNB, Not-for-Profit Organizations: A Legal Guide](https://www.pxw1.snb.ca/snb7001/e/1000/CSS-FOL-SNB-45-0014E.pdf).
- **Action (HIGH):** Before filing anything, confirm with SNB Corporate Registry (1-888-762-8600) whether any NFP modernization bill has since been introduced; treat "new NB NFP Act in force" as **false until proven**.

### 7.2 Federal CNCA — the solo-founder-friendly route
- **Finding:** Canada Not-for-profit Corporations Act (SC 2009, c 23): online incorporation ~**$200**, annual return ~**$12** (both historically accurate; current 2026 amounts UNVERIFIED); **minimum 1 director** for non-soliciting corporations, rising to **3 directors (2 non-officers/employees)** once "soliciting" (> $10K/yr in public donations or government grants); Canada-wide name protection; still requires **NB extra-provincial registration** via SNB (fee UNVERIFIED). Practitioners treat the CNCA as the preferred modern regime for NB nonprofits ([Blumbergs/CanadianCharityLaw.ca](https://www.canadiancharitylaw.ca/blog/good-news-for-alberta-and-new-brunswick-non-profits-that-wish-to-move-to-the-federal-canada-not-for-profit-corporations-act-cnca/)).
- **Sources:** [CNCA (Justice Laws)](https://laws-lois.justice.gc.ca/eng/acts/c-7.75/); [Corporations Canada, creating an NFP corporation](https://ised-isde.canada.ca/site/corporations-canada/en/not-profit-corporations/creating-not-profit-corporation); [SNB extra-provincial registration PDF](https://www.pxw1.snb.ca/snb7001/b/1000/CSS-FOL-SNB-45-0012B.pdf). Acc. 2026-07-14.
- **Implication:** CNCA is the only route a solo founder can start alone; the 3-director rule bites as soon as grants exceed $10K/yr — plan to recruit 2 more directors anyway.
- **Action (HIGH, when incorporating):** Default to CNCA online incorporation; verify current fees; budget NB extra-provincial registration.

### 7.3 Nonprofit vs. registered charity; minimum viable path
- **Finding:** Incorporation ≠ charity. **CRA charitable registration is separate** (no fee), needed for donation tax receipts and charity-only funding; "advancement of education" (Pemsel/CRA guidance CG-030) plausibly fits a structured free language-learning civic game, but no CRA precedent on a browser game was located (UNVERIFIED). Charity status adds T3010 filings and disbursement-quota compliance. Interim option: **fiscal sponsorship** with an existing NB nonprofit/charity (typical 5–10% admin fee — market convention, UNVERIFIED) gives immediate grant eligibility at zero incorporation cost. Most granting programs require incorporated status (verify per program).
- **Sources:** [CRA, applying for charitable registration](https://www.canada.ca/en/revenue-agency/services/charities-giving/charities/applying-registration.html) (currency UNVERIFIED); SNB Legal Guide (above).
- **Ranked path for a solo founder:** (1) stay unincorporated/fiscally sponsored until a specific funder requires otherwise; (2) CNCA (~$200, 1 director) when needed; (3) NB Companies Act (~$62 but 3 applicants, dated statute) only if a provincial brand is specifically preferred.
- **Actions:** (HIGH) Read one target funder's eligibility rules before structuring. (MED) Approach 1–2 Fredericton orgs about fiscal sponsorship; draft a CRA-model "advancement of education" purposes clause before incorporating (purposes are hard to change later). (LOW) Nuans pre-search/name reservation when incorporation becomes concrete.
